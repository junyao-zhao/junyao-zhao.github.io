Hi! I am an FSMP postdoctoral fellow at IRIF (CNRS & Université Paris Cité), hosted by Adrian Vladu. I did my Ph.D. in computer science at Stanford University, where I was very fortunate to be advised by Aviad Rubinstein. Before that, I obtained an M.Sc. with distinction in computer science from ETH Zürich and a B.Eng. in software engineering from Tongji University.

My research combines tools from theoretical computer science and game theory to analyze and design algorithms that make decisions and learn from feedback, and mechanisms that incentivize strategic agents to reach desirable equilibria. Specifically, I investigate fundamental questions such as:

  • Communication complexity: How much communication is necessary for optimal mechanisms to guide strategic agents toward desired social outcomes?
  • Simplicity vs. efficiency: How much economic or computational efficiency must we sacrifice if we require mechanisms to have simple forms?
  • Strategically robust learning: How can we design algorithms that learn reliably from feedback in repeated games without being manipulated?

In close connection to these topics, I am also interested in combinatorial optimization, especially problems involving submodular functions and matroids, which are central to modeling utility functions and the structure of feasible decisions.
